INSCONE: Unknown-Aware Detection of LLM-Generated Text via Informed Wild Data

Mark Stanley · Samad Syed · Masa Abboud · Saira Khatoon · Fairoz Khan
University of Wisconsin–Madison
Paper (PDF) Code Project page

Abstract

AI-generated text is increasingly difficult to distinguish from human writing, creating risks in academic integrity, medical misinformation, and social media disinformation. Recent work reframes machine-generated text (MGT) detection as an out-of-distribution problem with strong in-distribution and zero-shot results, but generalization to LLMs unseen during training remains underexplored.

We address this with two contributions. First, we propose INSCONE (Informed SCONE), which adapts the SCONE wild-data framework to the text domain by exploiting curated wild data with known mixing proportions to stabilize the energy geometry around seen and unseen LLM families, achieving strong zero-shot generalization on the RAID benchmark. Second, using Open-Set Recognition (OSR) as a base, we propose a 3-Zone Classification that simultaneously classifies known LLMs, unknown LLMs, and human text. We additionally characterize the adversarial robustness of the HTAO detector and extend the RAID benchmark with regenerations from contemporary frontier models, providing a broader empirical picture of detector behavior under realistic deployment conditions.

Key Insight

SCONE was designed for vision OSR: its uniform wild loss ReLU(−E)² pushes all wild samples toward high energy. In vision, this is harmless — covariate shift is pixel-level, and the Lipschitz drag from ID samples dominates. In text, "covariate shift" means an entirely different LLM family. The embedding distance from ID is large and heterogeneous, so the uniform push overwhelms the implicit drag and actively degrades zero-shot performance.

INSCONE's fix: when the wild batch is explicitly curated with known mixing proportions (πid, πc, πs), the quantile boundary between covariate and semantic OOD is exact and requires no estimation. We implement this as a proximal/distal split in energy space with a dead-zone buffer around the composition boundary — improving generalization with no architectural change to the base detector.

Method

Baseline: Energy-Based MGT Detector

Following Zeng et al. (2025), we use a frozen SimCSE-RoBERTa encoder with a 3-layer MLP head over K LLM family classes. The free energy score E(x) = −log Σᵢ exp(fᵢ(x)) is the anomaly signal: low energy = confident known-family prediction; high energy = human or unseen LLM text.

INSCONE: Informed Wild-Data Loss

Given known mixing proportions, we define proximal and distal thresholds on the wild energy distribution:

τ_prox  = Quantile(E_wild, 1 − π_s − δ/2)
τ_dist  = Quantile(E_wild, 1 − π_s + δ/2)

ℒ_INSCONE = ReLU(E_wild[E ≤ τ_prox] − m_in)²
          + ReLU(−E_wild[E ≥ τ_dist])²

The proximal term pulls estimated covariate OOD toward the ID energy region; the distal term pushes estimated semantic OOD toward high energy. Samples in the buffer (τ_prox, τ_dist) receive no gradient — preventing spurious updates from compositionally ambiguous samples.

Three-Zone OSR Investigation

While INSCONE optimizes binary AUROC, the stricter test is whether the energy classifier can perform fine-grained three-way classification: KNOWN family, COVARIATE machine text, and HUMAN. Vanilla SCONE achieves 99% AUROC on every pillar but only 70.6% macro Zone Accuracy. We apply four orthogonal interventions:

  • Grid-search calibration over (t_mid, t_out) on macro zone-accuracy — recovers latent zone separability already encoded in the trained energies (+18% absolute, no retraining).
  • SupervisedZoneDetector (SZD) — replaces quantile-based wild zone assignment with model-name-routed zones and directional energy targets (+6% absolute).
  • Outlier Exposure on covariate logits — adds KL(softmax ∥ uniform) to encourage genuine uncertainty on unfamiliar machine text (+3% absolute on unseen pillar).
  • Held-out dev-unseen calibration — final threshold selection on dev energies; zero-shot test is reserved for final reporting.

Adversarial Robustness

We evaluate HTAO's robustness under a black-box threat model: an adversary attempts to generate LLM text that evades detection with zero knowledge of detector architecture, weights, or threshold. The pretrained HTAO DeepSVDD checkpoint (RAID domain) serves as the target.

PhaseDescriptionFool RateBest Combo
Generation-time4 personas × 5 decoding configs (Qwen2.5-7B)0.0%N/A
Phase 112 post-hoc surface transforms10.1%70%
Phase 1.58 optimized stacked combinations16.1%96%
Phase 25 LLM rewriting strategies (Qwen2.5-1.5B)5.8%26%

Table 4: Adversarial results against HTAO DeepSVDD, all black-box. Generation-time attacks achieve 0% evasion, validating HTAO's core thesis that the OOD decision surface is robust to how text is generated. Stacked post-hoc surface transforms reach 96% on the best single combination.

Interpretation: The 96% fool rate from stacked typo + filler injection is not a failure of the OOD decision surface — these perturbations corrupt subword tokenization in ways that shift RoBERTa embeddings independently of semantic content. A human reader can trivially identify such texts as machine-generated and then corrupted. It exposes a tokenizer-level brittleness orthogonal to the semantic OOD framing, suggesting certified tokenizer robustness is a necessary complement to OOD-based detection.

RAID+ Extended Evaluation Set

We construct an extended evaluation set by regenerating RAID prompts with frontier models not available at HTAO publication: Gemini-2.5-Flash, DeepSeek-V3, and LLaMA-3.3-70B, among others, via LiteLLM (~2,000 samples per model, evaluation only). This supports reproducible benchmarking of detectors against contemporary LLMs.

RAID temporal split

ID: GPT-2, GPT-3, MPT, ChatGPT, LLaMA-Chat · Covariate: GPT-4, MPT-Chat · Zero-shot: Mistral, Cohere families · Frontier (eval): Gemini-2.5-Flash, DeepSeek-V3, LLaMA-3.3-70B
